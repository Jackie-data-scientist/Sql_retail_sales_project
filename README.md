# Retail Sales Analysis (SQL Project)

## 📊 Project Overview

This project focused on analyzing retail sales data using SQL. The dataset contains transaction-level records including customer demographics, product categories, sales amounts, and timestamps. The goal is to clean the data, perform exploratory data analysis, and extract business insights to support decision-making.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used

* SQL (MySQL)
* Data exploration and transformation techniques
* Window functions and aggregations

---

## 📁 Dataset Description

The dataset includes the following key fields:

* `transaction_id`
* `sale_date`
* `sale_time`
* `customer_id`
* `gender`
* `age`
* `category`
* `quantity`
* `price_per_unit`
* `cogs`
* `total_sale`

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning

The first step involved checking for missing values across all important columns:

* transactions_id
* sale_date
* sale_time
* customer_id
* gender
* age
* category
* quantity
* price_per_unit
* cogs
* total_sale

This ensured data integrity before analysis.

---

## 📊 Key Business Questions & Insights

### 1. Total Customers

* Counted total number of customers in the dataset.

### 2. Unique Customers

* Identified distinct customers to understand customer base size.

### 3. Total Sales

* Calculated overall revenue generated from transactions.

### 4. Product Categories

* Extracted all unique product categories.

---

## 🔍 Analytical Questions Solved

### 1. Sales on a Specific Date

Filtered all transactions on `2022-11-05`.

---

### 2. High Quantity Clothing Sales

Filtered clothing sales where quantity was 4 or more in November 2022.

---

### 3. Sales by Category

Calculated total sales and customer count per category.

---

### 4. Average Age of Beauty Customers

Computed average age of customers who purchased Beauty products.

---

### 5. High Value Transactions

Filtered transactions where total sales exceeded 1000.

---

### 6. Transactions by Gender and Category

Analyzed number of transactions across gender and product categories.

---

### 7. Monthly Sales Performance

Used window functions to identify the best-selling month in each year.

---

### 8. Top 5 Customers

Ranked customers based on total spending.

---

### 9. Unique Customers per Category

Identified how many unique customers purchased from each category.

---

### 10. Sales by Time Shift

Segmented sales into:

* Morning (<12)
* Afternoon (12–17)
* Evening (>17)

and counted orders per shift.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

* Sales vary significantly by category and time of day.
* A small group of customers contributes a large portion of revenue.
* Certain months show peak performance within each year.
* Customer demographics (age and gender) influence category preferences.

---

## 🚀 Future Improvements

* Add visualizations using Power BI or Tableau
* Perform customer segmentation (RFM analysis)
* Automate reporting using Python or SQL scripts
